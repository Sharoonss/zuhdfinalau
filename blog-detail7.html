Understanding Qadr – Finding Peace in Allah's Plan

Date: August 27, 2025

Introduction

Belief in Qadr (Divine Decree) is one of the six pillars of faith in Islam. It is the conviction that everything—good or bad, ease or hardship—happens by the will and wisdom of Allah ﷻ. For many, Qadr is one of the most challenging aspects of faith to understand, yet it is also the key to inner peace.

The Qur'an and Sunnah teach us that accepting Allah's plan brings comfort to the heart, prevents despair, and strengthens patience. In this article, we will explore the meaning of Qadr, its wisdom, and how to find peace in Allah's decree through Qur'anic verses and authentic Ahadith from Sahih Bukhari, Sahih Muslim, and Sunan al-Tirmidhi.

Carousel Snapshot (Quick Reminders)

  1. Belief in Qadr is Part of Iman
  2. Everything Happens for a Wisdom
  3. Qadr Does Not Negate Effort
  4. Trials are a Test of Faith
  5. Finding Peace in Allah's Plan

Conclusion

Understanding Qadr is not about knowing every detail of Allah's decree but about trusting His wisdom. When we accept Qadr, we free ourselves from constant anxiety and instead gain contentment in Allah's plan. The Qur'an and Sunnah remind us that Qadr includes both good and trials, but for the believer, both are ultimately a source of reward.

By believing in Qadr, striving with effort, and showing patience in hardship, we can find peace in every stage of life. May Allah grant us hearts that are firm in faith, content with His decree, and always grateful in ease and hardship. Ameen.
